10-Year Anniversary Campaign

Lifecycle Strategy Analysis — Informed by Historical Campaign Performance
Prepared May 12, 2026 • Campaign Window: June 13–28, 2026

Executive Summary

The 10-Year Anniversary Campaign (June 13–28, 2026) is the most comprehensive lifecycle effort Prehab has ever run. This document lays out what we're building, why, and what data from previous campaigns backs each decision.

What We Learned From
$482K

What We're Building
15
lifecycle journeys across Prehab App + Professional
Key Learning
In-App
Embedded & in-app have the highest measurable engagement rates
What this document covers: We analyzed every Iterable campaign from July 4th 2025 (44 campaigns), BFCM 2025 (52 campaigns), and NPTM 2025 (15 campaigns) — then cross-referenced engagement data against ClickHouse conversion and revenue data. The findings directly inform which journeys to run, how many touchpoints, and which channels to prioritize for the 10-Year campaign.

Three things the data told us

  1. Embedded and in-app messaging have the highest measurable conversion rates. Embedded Home Screen click rates (6–12%) significantly outperform tracked email CTR (0.2–0.5%). In-app messages had up to 47% open rates. Note: some email campaigns used deep links (especially SalesUp and Web2App journeys), which prevents click tracking — actual email engagement for those journeys may be higher than reported. Every app-facing journey needs embedded + in-app creative.
  2. Email is the volume driver. Email reaches 78K–114K unique recipients per campaign and puts the offer in front of the broadest audience. For non-member journeys (where links go to web, not deep links), email CTR is trackable and consistent at 0.2–0.3%. For in-app journeys using deep links, email engagement may be underreported.
  3. Pro lifecycle works — and has long-tail value. Of 2,467 NPTM email clickers, 404 matched to Pro accounts and 391 are still active. 39 clickers paid $4,733 during the campaign window, but the real value is retention: 30 people who opened a campaign email in October didn't sign up until months later — 97% are still paying. This is the first time we're running Pro lifecycle alongside App in the same campaign.

Journey-by-Journey Recommendations

Each journey is categorized as MUST RUN (strong historical evidence), RUN (logical extension of proven patterns), or NEW BET (no direct data, but low effort / high upside).

Non-Members App (5 emails + embedded + push)
MUST RUN
Largest audience, largest absolute conversion count in both J4 (214 IC, 18% of total) and BFCM (217 IC). The backbone of every campaign. 50.5% account-to-IC CVR when in workflow vs. 26.7% outside — the workflow demonstrably works.
Channel performance from ClickHouse:
  • Email: J4: 7 emails, 525K sends, 8.6% OR, 0.2% CTR. BFCM: 5 emails, 542K sends, 8.5% OR, 0.3% CTR. Volume driver but low per-email click rate.
  • Embedded: J4 had 1,437 impressions per placement (Home, Settings, Membership). Home Screen had highest click rate at 6.1% (87 clickers). Settings and Membership ~1.7–1.8%.
  • Push: J4 had 4 push campaigns with 80 total unique openers (23, 22, 19, 16 each). BFCM had 4 push campaigns with 116 total unique openers (41, 29, 26, 20 each). Low rates (0.1–0.2%) but non-zero reach.
Strategy implication: Non-member email is the volume driver (highest absolute reach). But for users with the app installed, embedded messaging (6.1% Home Screen click rate) significantly outperforms email (0.2% CTR). Add embedded creative on Home Screen + Manage Membership. Push adds marginal reach. Front-load best email content — first sends have highest OR/CTR.
InApp SalesUp (3 emails + embedded + push)
MUST RUN
Second biggest contributor in J4 (143 IC, 12% of total). BFCM A/B test proved lifecycle lift: 42 conversions in test group (80%) vs. 15 in control (20%). These are high-intent users who just created an account.
Channel performance from ClickHouse (Activation SalesUp / AppSalesUp):
  • Embedded: J4 had 595 impressions per placement, 33 unique clickers (3.7% Home, 2.2% Membership, 1.2% Settings). 6 of 33 clickers currently paid (18.2%). BFCM: 406 impressions, 29 clickers (4.9% Home, 2.7% Settings). 7 of 29 currently paid (24.1%).
  • Push: J4 had 42 unique push openers across 7 campaigns — 10 currently paid (23.8%). Best: BodyScan_1 and AtRisk_9 each drove 12 openers. BFCM had 77 push openers — 8 currently paid (10.4%). First two pushes drove most opens (29 and 25).
  • Email: J4 had 13.7% OR, 0.6% CTR. BFCM had 6.7% OR, 0.0% tracked CTR — however, deep links in SalesUp emails likely prevented click tracking, so actual clicks are unknown.
Strategy implication: Embedded and push have measurable engagement for SalesUp users (4–5% embedded click rate, 77 push openers in BFCM). Email CTR for SalesUp may be understated due to deep link tracking limitations. The 10-Year plan's 3 emails should be supplemented with embedded messaging on Home Screen + Membership and 2–3 push notifications (early pushes drive 2x the opens of later ones). These users are already in the app; meet them there.
Left in Cart — App (3 emails)
MUST RUN
Highest per-user CVR of any journey in BFCM: 12.7% (181 conversions from 1,423 audience). J4 had only 21 entries (low cart traffic), but the intent signal is the strongest in the funnel. These users were one click from purchasing.
Recommendation: Must run. Ensure checkout abandonment tracking is airtight. The 30-min delay on Email #1 is exactly right — catch them while the tab is still open.
App Reactivation (4 emails + 3 pushes + embedded + in-app)
MUST RUN
J4: 68 winbacks (6% of IC). Highest email click-to-IC of any journey at 16.36%. Premium/lapsed audience responds better to lifecycle messaging than any other segment. BFCM: 34 conversions from 2,779 audience.
Channel performance from ClickHouse (Winback):
  • Embedded: J4: 651 impressions per placement. Home Screen 9.8% click rate, Membership 8.5%, Settings 4.3% — highest embedded click rates of any journey. BFCM: 427 impressions. Membership 8.9%, Home 7.7%, Settings 6.4%.
  • In-App: J4: 596 users opened winback in-app. BFCM: 331 users opened. BFCM in-app winback drove $2,942 from 23 payers — highest per-user revenue of any channel/journey combo. 36.6% of all BFCM in-app openers are currently paid.
  • Email: J4: 12.6% OR, 1.3% CTR. BFCM: 10.4% OR, 0.0% tracked CTR (may be affected by deep link tracking limitations).
Strategy implication: Winback is where embedded and in-app shine brightest. Embedded Membership placement (8.5–8.9% click rate) and in-app messages ($2,942 BFCM revenue) are critical channels. Must include embedded + in-app for the 10-Year Reactivation journey — these lapsed users still have the app and respond to in-context prompts far better than email.
Monthly Member Expansion — App (3 emails + 3 pushes + embedded + in-app)
MUST RUN
Small in absolute terms (14 upgrades in J4, 4 in BFCM) but the workflow drove 71% of all upgrades in J4. These are paying users — every upgrade is pure retention value with zero acquisition cost.
Channel performance from ClickHouse (Monthly Expansion):
  • Embedded: J4: 110 impressions per placement. Home Screen had 11.8% click rate (13 clickers) — highest of any embedded placement across all journeys. BFCM: 64 impressions. Settings 7.8%, Membership 6.3%, Home 4.7%.
  • In-App: J4: 102 users opened Monthly in-app message. BFCM: 54 openers. BFCM in-app Monthly had 47.4% open rate (from Iterable metrics) — highest of any in-app message.
  • Email: J4: 9.9% OR, 0.5% CTR. BFCM: 11.0% OR, 0.0% tracked CTR (may be affected by deep link tracking limitations).
  • Push: BFCM Monthly push had 3.4% OR (11 opens from 323 sent) — 2nd highest push open rate after AppSalesUp test.
Strategy implication: Embedded Home Screen (11.8% click rate) and in-app (47.4% open rate) do the heavy lifting here, not email. Embedded and in-app are non-negotiable for Monthly Expansion. Email serves as a reminder; the conversion happens in-app.

RUN

Strategic Principles (Data-Backed)

Six principles derived from J4 and BFCM performance data that should guide execution of the 10-Year campaign.

1
Front-load your best content J4 data: first emails had the highest open rates and CTR across all journeys. The 10-Year plan's deal launch on Day 1 (June 19) is correct. Don't save your best creative for the end.
2
Embedded messaging is the highest-converting channel 22–38% click-to-conversion across all J4 journeys. Low effort (already implemented evergreen). Make sure campaign-specific embedded creative is live for every segment that has app access: InApp SalesUp, Monthly Expansion, Reactivation, Winback.
3
LIC is your highest-ROI journey per user 12.7% CVR in BFCM vs. ~1% for non-member sends. Ensure checkout abandonment tracking is verified and working for both App and Pro before June 19. One broken event = hundreds of missed conversions.
4
Fresh emails convert best BFCM: 51% of journey-attributed conversions came from emails collected during the campaign window itself. The event-triggered journeys (InApp SalesUp, Quiz SalesUp, LIC) are designed to catch these users. Prioritize their build.
5
In-app conversions dominate — even from email CTAs J4: 55–62% of conversions happened in-app across all journeys, even when email CTAs pointed to the website. Drive users to the app when possible. Deep links > web links.
6
Premium audiences respond better than non-premium Winback email click-to-IC was 16.4% vs. 4% for non-members. Monthly expansion email sent-to-IC was 4.55% vs. 0.35% for non-members. Allocate creative effort accordingly — personalization matters more for smaller, higher-intent segments.

Risks & Open Items

1. Pro Event Triggers: This is the first campaign with full Pro lifecycle journeys (Quiz SalesUp, LIC, Reactivation, Pending Cancel Winback). All of these depend on Iterable events firing correctly for Pro — verify account_created, checkout_abandoned, subscription_cancelled, and quiz_completed events exist and are mapped for Prehab Professional before launch.
2. Pro Team Expansion Copy Mismatch: The journey title is 10Yr_TeamExpansion_Pro but the email copy in the sheet pitches monthly → annual upgrade pricing ($100 vs. $144). Craig's intent is individual → team expansion. Copy needs to be rewritten to match.
3. Suppression Logic Complexity: With 15 journeys across two products running simultaneously, suppression rules are critical. Active App members must not get non-member App emails. Active Pro subscribers must not get non-member Pro emails. Users tagged via the enrichment email must route correctly. A suppression matrix should be built and QA'd.
4. Pro Performance Data Now Available: Comprehensive NPTM 2025 analysis shows 2,467 email clickers, 404 matched to Pro users (391 active), and $4,733 in Stripe revenue from clickers during the campaign window. Pro Group emails had 29.6% open rate. Still only one prior Pro campaign to benchmark against — set expectations that most new Pro journeys (Quiz, LIC, Reactivation, Pending Cancel) are baseline-setting.
5. Email Fatigue Across Journeys: A non-member who clicks the Pro CTA in the enrichment email could receive: 2 announcement emails + 5 Pro non-member emails + potentially a quiz or LIC sequence. That's 7–10 emails in 15 days. Monitor unsubscribe rates after Day 3 and have a throttle plan.

Final Verdict

Run all 15 journeys. Lifecycle emails reached 27–33% of App Stripe payers during J4 and BFCM, and 16% of Pro Stripe payers during NPTM. Across $482K in campaign revenue, the data is clear: embedded and in-app channels drive the highest per-user conversion rates, email drives volume, and Pro lifecycle has strong long-tail value. The 10-Year plan builds on every proven journey type, adds Pro-side equivalents backed by comprehensive NPTM data, and introduces smarter segmentation via the enrichment email. The plan is tighter than both J4 and BFCM — fewer emails per journey, better channel mix, and the first dual-product campaign.

The three things that will determine whether this campaign outperforms J4 and BFCM:

  1. Enrichment email click-through rate — If it works, non-members get product-relevant messaging from Day 1 instead of generic blasts. That's a structural advantage no prior campaign had.
  2. Pro event trigger reliability — Six new Pro journeys depend on events that may not have been used in Iterable before. One broken trigger = an entire journey goes dark.
  3. Embedded messaging coverage — The highest-converting channel in J4. Make sure campaign-specific embedded creative is live across all app-facing segments, not just Monthly Expansion.
